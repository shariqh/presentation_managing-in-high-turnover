I know I said it's not about numbers, but...

Almost two-thirds of engineers who recently left their jobs said they would have stayed had their employer taken steps to create a more positive and respectful work environment
While 52% of employees exiting voluntarily said their manager or organization could have done something to prevent them from leaving their job
This means that we are missing the opportunities to support and make the lives of those around us better 🚀

So here's some easy tips 🤑

No, not cash tips
Be organized and detailed from onboarding to role-off Onboarding should include specific role expectations (in addition to the other organizational information e.g. org tree, helpful tips, basic culture info)
Assign a mentor/coach who is not the direct manager No one feels good bothering a direct manager about every question - especially technical ones. Lean on the team leads and more senior team members
Set up touch points often One on one time is underrated. Try to talk to team members weekly and ask them questions about their current roles, accomplishments and where they would like help
Document everything and over communicate It is very important that all relevant team members know what is being worked on, but also that their accomplishments are documented timely
